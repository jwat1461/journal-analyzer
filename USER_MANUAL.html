Overview

AI Recovery Tracker is a self-hosted web application running at http://localhost:3001. All data is stored in a private PostgreSQL database — no cloud, no tracking, no data sharing. It has three integrated suites:

📝
Journaling Suite

Daily entries, mood tracking, habit grid, AI insights, file manager, and calendar integration.

💙
Recovery Suite

Sobriety counter, meeting tracker, 90-in-90, sponsor tools, 12-step progress with timestamped notes, daily tasks, and quit habits.

🤲
Sponsor Dashboard

Track your sponsees — step progress, contact info, notes — all in one private place.

🔗
Resources Page

Built-in links to NA.org, AA.org, SAMHSA, 988 Lifeline, and more. Add your own custom links.

Profile & Program Selector New v3

The top of the sidebar has two new personalisation features:

Profile Picture

The circular avatar at the top-left of the sidebar shows your profile picture. By default it shows the app emoji (📓). To change it:

  1. Click (or tap) the avatar circle — a 📷 overlay appears on hover.

Your avatar is stored on the server at uploads/avatar.jpg and persists across browser sessions. To remove it, use the server directly to delete the file.

Add Entry

Add Entry — write, pick habits, add tags

  1. Click Add Entry in the sidebar.
  2. Type in the text area. Word count updates live.
  3. Select a mood (1–5) at the top.
  4. Click any habit badge to log that habit for today.
  5. Add tags by typing and pressing Enter.
  6. Click Save Entry.

Import Entries

Drag and drop a CSV or TXT file to bulk-import past entries. CSV format: date, content, mood.

Habits Tracker

Habits — 30-day grid with today's column

The Habits page shows a 30-day rolling grid. Filled (cyan) cells = completed. The rightmost column (●) is today — click to toggle.

Go to Settings (⚙️ sidebar) to add or remove habits. Each habit has a name, colour, and optional emoji.

Calendar

Calendar — monthly view with events and journal markers

Monthly grid showing journal entry markers (📓) and coloured calendar events. Click any day to see its events and journal entry in a popup.

Creating an Event

  1. Click + New Event or click directly on a calendar day.
  2. Enter title, date, time, and colour.
  3. Click Save.
Tip — Auto Calendar from Meetings
When adding a meeting in the Meetings tab, check "Add to calendar automatically" and the app will create 8 weekly recurring calendar events for you automatically.

Sobriety Counter

The Recovery Counter card sits at the top of the Dashboard and tracks exactly how long you have been clean.

Setting Your Clean Date

  1. Click ✏️ Set Date on the Recovery Counter card.
  2. Enter the date you got clean in the date picker.
  3. Click Save. The counter updates immediately and starts ticking live.

The counter displays years / months / days clean, total day count, a live HH:MM:SS ticker, your clean date, and a daily motivational quote.

Secure Storage
Your clean date is stored in the PostgreSQL database — never in the browser. It persists across devices that connect to the same server.

NA Meetings & 90-in-90 Recovery

Meetings — 7-day grid with attendance, 90-in-90 tracker, and stats modal

Weekly Meeting Grid

Seven columns represent the current week (today highlighted in blue). Each meeting card shows time, name, location, and commitment type.

Adding a Meeting

  1. Click + Add Meeting or the + Add under any day column.
  2. Enter the name, day of week, time, and location.
  3. Choose a commitment type: Member, Chair, Read, Greet, Share, or Speaker.
  4. Pick a colour swatch for the card.
  5. Toggle Recurring for weekly meetings.
  6. Optionally check "Add to calendar automatically" to create calendar events.
  7. Click Save Meeting.

Marking Attendance

Click Mark Attended on a meeting card to log attendance for today. The button turns green. Click again to un-mark.

Meeting Attendance Stats New

Each meeting card has a small 📊 button in the top-right corner. Click it to open the Meeting Stats modal, which shows:

  • Total Attended — all-time count for this specific meeting
  • This Month — attendance count for the current month
  • This Week — attendance count since Sunday
  • 🔥 badge if you have any attendance logged

90 Meetings in 90 Days New

The 90-in-90 Tracker card sits above the weekly grid. It helps you complete the traditional newcomer commitment of attending 90 NA meetings in 90 days.

  • Click 🔥 Start 90-in-90 to begin. Today's date is set as the start date.
  • The tracker counts any attendance dates logged within the 90-day window.
  • Four stat counters show: Attended, Days Left, To Catch Up, and Progress %.
  • A progress bar turns green when complete and shows orange when behind pace.
  • The Reset button starts the counter over from scratch.
Tip
The status badge shows On Track (green) when you've attended ≥ the number of days elapsed, or Behind (orange) when you need to catch up. Once you hit 90 attended meetings it shows ✅ Complete!

Sponsor & 12-Step Progress Recovery

Sponsor & Recovery — sponsor profile and 12-step grid

My Sponsor

Click ✏️ Edit Sponsor to enter your sponsor's name, phone, email, years clean, and notes. Once saved, the sponsor card shows tap-to-call (📞) and tap-to-text (💬) buttons.

12-Step Progress Grid

A 6×2 grid shows all 12 steps. Completed steps are filled blue. Your current step shows a gold border. Click any step to open the Step Detail panel.

Working a Step

  1. Click any step box in the grid.
  2. The step modal opens with the step title, description, and a notes textarea.
  3. Type a note or reflection in the Add a Note field.
  4. Click Save — your note is timestamped and saved. The textarea clears for another note.
  5. When ready, check Mark this step complete and click Save.

Completing a step automatically advances your current step to the next incomplete one.

Reopen a Completed Step

If a step was completed but needs more work, click ↩ Reopen Step in the green banner at the top of the step modal. Save to confirm.

Step Notes History New v3

Each step now keeps a timestamped notes history — every note you add is saved as a separate entry (not overwriting the previous one).

How it works

  • Type in the "Add a Note" textarea and click Save. The note is stored with today's date.
  • Previous notes appear below under "Previous Notes" in reverse-chronological order.
  • Each note entry shows its date and a ✕ delete button.
  • You can add as many notes per step as you like — they all accumulate over time.
  • Saving with no note text (only the completion checkbox) closes the modal immediately.
Use Case
Use step notes like a step-work journal. Write your daily reflections, inventory items, or sponsor feedback for each step — all with a date stamp so you can see your progress over time.

Daily Recovery Tasks Recovery

The Daily Recovery Tasks section lives on the Sponsor page below the 12-step grid. Seven default tasks are pre-loaded (call sponsor, pray, read, meetings, etc.).

Completing Tasks

Check the checkbox to mark a task done for today. Completions show a strikethrough and advance the progress bar. Checkboxes reset automatically each day at midnight.

Adding / Removing Tasks

Click + Add Task to add a custom task. Click the ✕ button beside any task to remove it permanently.

Quick Access from Dashboard
The NA Recovery Dashboard widget shows your top tasks with checkboxes — check them off without leaving the Dashboard.

Quit Habits Tracker New v3

The Quit Tracker card sits below Daily Tasks on the Sponsor page. Track anything you've quit — smoking, alcohol, social media, gambling, caffeine — each with its own sobriety-style streak counter.

Adding a Quit Habit

  1. Click + Add Habit on the Quit Tracker card.
  2. Enter what you quit (e.g. "Smoking", "Vaping", "Alcohol").
  3. Set the Quit Date — the date you stopped.
  4. Pick a colour for the streak card.
  5. Optionally add a note about why you quit or how you feel.
  6. Click Save.

Each quit habit card shows:

  • Days clean — calculated live from the quit date ("365 days clean", "Day 1 — You got this!")
  • Progress bar — percent of the first 365 days
  • Notes — your personal note below the bar

Editing or Deleting

Click the ✏️ button on any card to open the edit modal. Click Delete (red) to remove it permanently.

Sponsee Tracking New v3

The My Sponsees card sits at the bottom of the Sponsor page. If you sponsor others in recovery, this is where you track their progress.

Adding a Sponsee

  1. Click + Add Sponsee.
  2. Enter their name (first name or initials — as private as you need).
  3. Optionally enter a phone number for a tap-to-call link.
  4. Select their Current Step from the dropdown.
  5. Add any notes (meeting frequency, progress, goals).
  6. Click Save.

Each sponsee card shows:

  • Avatar circle with their initial
  • Step progress badge (e.g. "Step 4") and step progress bar
  • Tap-to-call phone link (if a number is saved)
  • Notes text below

Updating Progress

Click the ✏️ button on a sponsee card to open the edit modal. Update their current step and notes anytime. Click Remove (red) to delete the record.

Data & Privacy

All data is stored in your local PostgreSQL database. Nothing is shared with external servers except when you click Generate Insights (which sends journal text to the Anthropic Claude API).

Your Data is Private
Journal entries, sobriety date, meeting schedules, sponsor information, step notes, quit habit records, sponsee records, and resource links are all stored in your local PostgreSQL database only. The app runs entirely on your own machine.
